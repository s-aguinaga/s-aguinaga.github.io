About

I am a PhD candidate in CS at the University of Notre Dame. My research centers on developing mathematical abstractions and algorithms to study complex information networks. Currently I am working on generative network models that combines methods in natural language processing with graph theory to learn the grammars of networks. Previous work includes stints in auditory research and electro-physiology at Northwestern University as well as stints as a hardware EE at Motorola (mobile devices) and 3Com (now, HPE Networking).
In my free time you’ll find me fixing my house, running, sailing (less frequently nowawdays), and spending time with friends, but most importantly with family, who are always reminding me to maintain a good sense of humor.
